Emergency Hotlines

Fever - is a temporary increase in your body temperature, often due to an illness.

Cough - also known as tussis, is a voluntary or involuntary act that clears the throat and breathing passage of foreign particles, microbes, irritants, fluids, and mucus; it is a rapid expulsion of air from the lungs.

Headache - is pain in any region of the head.

Diarrhea - is loose, watery stools (bowel movements). You have diarrhea if you have loose stools three or more times in one day.

Sore Throat - is pain, scratchiness or irritation of the throat that often worsens when you swallow.

Muscle Pain - is caused by viral infections, including COVID-19, can happen after a virus stimulates your immune system. Muscle Pain is most often related to tension, overuse, or muscle injury from exercise or hard physical work.

Shortness of Breath - is often described as an intense tightening in the chest, air hunger, difficulty breathing, breathlessness or a feeling of suffocation.

Loss of Taste & Smell - loss of smell can occur suddenly in people with COVID-19 and is often accompanied by loss of taste. Also, with COVID-19, these symptoms may occur without a runny or stuffy nose.
